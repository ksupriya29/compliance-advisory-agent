# International Data Transfers Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs the transfer of personal data from the European Economic
Area (EEA) to third countries, including the United States. It implements
the requirements of Chapter V of the UK GDPR and EU GDPR and applies to all
processing activities involving personal data of EEA or UK data subjects.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all transfers of personal data to:
- Group entities located outside the EEA or UK
- Third-party processors or sub-processors in third countries
- Cloud service providers operating infrastructure outside the EEA or UK

## 3. Prohibition on Unlawful Transfers

Personal data of EU or UK data subjects must not be transferred to a country
outside the EEA or UK unless one of the approved transfer mechanisms set out
in Section 4 is in place prior to the transfer commencing. Transfers to the
United States or any other third country without an approved mechanism are
**prohibited**.

## 4. Approved Transfer Mechanisms

### 4.1 Adequacy Decision

Transfers may proceed without additional safeguards to countries that benefit
from a UK or EU adequacy decision. The DPO maintains a current list of
adequate countries. The adequacy status of any destination country must be
verified with the DPO before transfer.

### 4.2 Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)

Where no adequacy decision applies, transfers to third countries may be made
on the basis of the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
or the UK's International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA). The relevant clauses
must be executed in full before data transfer commences.

A Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) must be completed for all SCC-based
transfers to assess whether the laws of the destination country undermine the
protections provided by the SCCs.

### 4.3 Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)

Intra-group transfers may be made on the basis of approved Binding Corporate
Rules where the relevant supervisory authority has approved the BCRs. The DPO
holds copies of all approved BCRs and must be consulted before relying on
this mechanism.

### 4.4 EU–US Data Privacy Framework

Transfers to US-based organisations that are certified under the EU–US Data
Privacy Framework (DPF) are permitted without additional safeguards for
transfers from the EU. For UK transfers, the UK Extension to the DPF applies.
Certification status must be verified on the DPF list at the time of transfer
and annually thereafter.

## 5. Storing EU Customer Data in the US Region

Storing EU customer personal data on infrastructure located in the United States
requires one of the mechanisms in Section 4 to be in place. Specifically:

- If using a US cloud provider (e.g. AWS us-east-1, Azure East US, GCP
  us-central1), the organisation must have executed SCCs (Controller-to-
  Processor) with the provider, completed a Transfer Impact Assessment, and
  implemented supplementary technical measures (encryption at rest and in
  transit, with keys held in the EEA) where the TIA identifies residual risk.
- Alternatively, the provider must hold a valid DPF certification covering the
  categories of data being transferred.

The DPO must approve any new arrangement to store EU customer data in the US
region before the arrangement goes live. Evidence of the approved transfer
mechanism must be documented and retained.

Storing EU customer data in the US region without an approved transfer mechanism
in place constitutes a violation of Article 46 UK/EU GDPR and may result in
regulatory enforcement action, fines of up to **4% of global annual turnover**,
and reputational harm.

## 6. Data Minimisation for Cross-Border Transfers

Only the minimum personal data necessary for the legitimate purpose may be
transferred. Special category data (health, biometric, racial or ethnic
origin data) must not be transferred to third countries unless explicit consent
has been obtained or a specific legal derogation under Article 49 GDPR applies,
and the DPO has given prior approval.

## 7. Third-Party Processor Due Diligence

Before engaging any processor located outside the EEA or UK, the procurement
team must complete a third-party data protection assessment and obtain DPO
sign-off. A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) incorporating the applicable SCCs
or IDTA must be executed prior to data sharing.

## 8. Incident and Breach Notification

If personal data is transferred to a third country without an approved
mechanism, this must be treated as a personal data breach and reported to
the DPO within **one (1) hour** of discovery. The DPO will determine
whether notification to the relevant supervisory authority is required within
the 72-hour window under Article 33 GDPR.

## 9. Annual Review

This policy must be reviewed annually by the DPO and updated to reflect
changes in adequacy decisions, new SCCs or IDTA versions, and any changes to
the DPF or BCR approvals.
