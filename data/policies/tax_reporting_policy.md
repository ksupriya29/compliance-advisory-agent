# Tax Reporting and Regulatory Filing Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out the organisation's obligations and internal procedures
for tax reporting, regulatory filings, and related record-keeping. It applies
to all staff with responsibilities in finance, compliance, and operations.

## 2. Scope

This policy covers:
- Corporate tax returns and VAT filings
- Payroll tax (PAYE/NIC) reporting
- Country-by-Country Reporting (CbCR)
- Common Reporting Standard (CRS) and FATCA filings
- Any other regulatory reporting obligations imposed by law

## 3. Corporate Tax Obligations

### 3.1 Annual Tax Return

The organisation must file a Corporation Tax return (CT600) with HMRC within
**twelve (12) months** of the end of the accounting period. Tax due must be
paid within **nine (9) months and one day** of the period end.

The Finance team is responsible for engaging the external tax adviser no later
than **three (3) months** before the filing deadline.

### 3.2 VAT Returns

VAT-registered entities must submit quarterly VAT returns via Making Tax
Digital (MTD)-compatible software within **one (1) month and seven (7) days**
of the end of each VAT quarter. Late filing attracts automatic penalties.

### 3.3 Country-by-Country Reporting

Where the organisation's consolidated group revenue exceeds £750 million, a
Country-by-Country Report must be filed with HMRC within **twelve (12) months**
of the end of the fiscal year. The CbCR must include revenue, profit before
tax, income tax paid, and employee headcount by jurisdiction.

## 4. Payroll Tax Reporting

### 4.1 Real Time Information (RTI)

All payroll payments must be reported to HMRC on or before the date of payment
via Full Payment Submission (FPS) under the Real Time Information (RTI) system.
Failure to file on time will result in automatic late-filing penalties.

### 4.2 P60 and P11D

- **P60**: Must be issued to every employee still in employment at 5 April, by
  **31 May** following the end of the tax year.
- **P11D**: Must be filed with HMRC by **6 July** following the end of the tax
  year for all employees receiving benefits in kind.

## 5. CRS and FATCA Reporting

### 5.1 Common Reporting Standard

The organisation is a Reporting Financial Institution under the OECD Common
Reporting Standard. It must identify reportable accounts, collect self-
certification from account holders, and submit the annual CRS return to HMRC
by **31 May** each year, covering the preceding calendar year.

### 5.2 FATCA

Where the organisation has US reportable accounts, a FATCA report must be
submitted to HMRC (for onward transmission to the IRS) by **31 May** each
year. The report must cover all US persons holding financial accounts with
a balance or value exceeding the applicable threshold.

## 6. Internal Controls

All tax filings must be reviewed and approved by the Head of Finance before
submission. A second sign-off from the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) is
required for any filing where the tax liability or penalty exposure exceeds
**£100,000**.

## 7. Record-Keeping

All tax-related records, including workings, correspondence with HMRC, and
filed returns, must be retained for **seven (7) years** from the end of the
relevant accounting period (see Data Retention Policy §3.4).

## 8. Penalties and Escalation

Any late filing, error, or potential penalty must be escalated to the Head of
Tax and the Legal team within **24 hours** of identification. The Compliance
team must be notified of any HMRC enquiry or investigation within **48 hours**
of receipt of the initial HMRC communication.
