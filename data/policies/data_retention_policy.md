# Data Retention Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the minimum and maximum retention periods for all categories
of customer and operational records held by the organisation. It applies to all
business units and all media (paper, electronic, cloud-hosted).

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party processors
who handle data on behalf of the organisation.

## 3. Retention Periods by Record Type

### 3.1 Customer KYC and Identity Records

Customer Know-Your-Customer (KYC) records, including copies of identity
documents, proof of address, and beneficial ownership declarations, must be
retained for a minimum of **five (5) years** from the date the business
relationship ends or the date of the transaction, whichever is later.

Extended retention of up to **ten (10) years** is required where:
- The record relates to a suspicious activity report (SAR) filed with the
  relevant financial intelligence unit.
- A regulatory investigation or legal proceeding is ongoing or anticipated.

### 3.2 Transaction Records

All transaction records, including payment instructions, settlement records,
and account statements, must be retained for **five (5) years** from the
date of the transaction.

### 3.3 Customer Communication Records

Emails, chat logs, and written correspondence relating to customer complaints,
regulatory matters, or contractual disputes must be retained for **six (6)
years** from the date of the last communication.

### 3.4 Employee Records

Personnel files, payroll records, and disciplinary records must be retained
for **six (6) years** after the employment relationship ends.

Tax-related payroll records must be retained for **seven (7) years** to satisfy
HMRC obligations.

### 3.5 Regulatory Filings and Reports

All regulatory submissions, including suspicious activity reports (SARs),
currency transaction reports (CTRs), and annual regulatory returns, must be
retained for **five (5) years** from the date of filing.

### 3.6 Audit Logs and Access Logs

System audit logs and access control logs must be retained for **twelve (12)
months** in active storage and a further **24 months** in cold/archive storage,
for a total of **three (3) years**.

## 4. Data Deletion Obligations

Records must be securely deleted or anonymised promptly upon expiry of the
applicable retention period, unless:
- A legal hold has been placed on the record.
- The record is subject to an active regulatory investigation.
- Deletion would conflict with another applicable legal obligation.

Deletion must be documented. The organisation must maintain a deletion log
showing the record category, approximate volume, deletion date, and method used.

## 5. Data Subject Rights and Retention

Where a data subject exercises their Right to Erasure under Article 17 GDPR,
records may be retained beyond the subject's request only where retention is
required by applicable law (e.g. AML obligations under the Money Laundering
Regulations). In such cases, the data must be restricted from further
processing and the data subject must be informed of the legal basis for
continued retention.

## 6. Responsibility

The Data Protection Officer (DPO) is responsible for maintaining the Records
Retention Schedule and reviewing it annually. Line managers are responsible for
ensuring their teams comply with this policy.
