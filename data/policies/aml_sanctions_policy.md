# Anti-Money Laundering and Sanctions Compliance Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the organisation's obligations and internal controls
for preventing money laundering, terrorist financing, and sanctions violations.
It implements the requirements of the Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and
Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017, the Proceeds of
Crime Act 2002, the Terrorism Act 2000, and applicable sanctions regimes
including those administered by OFAC, the UN Security Council, HM Treasury
(OFSI), and the EU.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and agents involved in
onboarding customers, processing transactions, monitoring accounts, or
performing compliance functions.

## 3. Know Your Customer and Customer Due Diligence

### 3.1 Standard Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

Standard CDD must be performed before establishing any business relationship
or executing any occasional transaction above €15,000 (or equivalent). CDD
must include:
- Verification of the customer's identity using reliable, independent source documents
- Identification and verification of the beneficial owner (UBO) where ownership
  or control exceeds 25%
- Understanding the nature and purpose of the intended business relationship

### 3.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Enhanced Due Diligence is mandatory for:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their close associates and family members
- Customers or counterparties from high-risk third countries designated by the
  Financial Action Task Force (FATF)
- Correspondent banking relationships
- Any customer or transaction presenting higher money laundering or terrorist
  financing risk as assessed by the firm's risk appetite framework

EDD must include senior management approval before onboarding, source-of-funds
and source-of-wealth verification, and enhanced ongoing monitoring.

### 3.3 Simplified Due Diligence (SDD)

Simplified Due Diligence may only be applied where the firm has determined,
through documented risk assessment, that the business relationship or
transaction presents a low risk of money laundering or terrorist financing.
SDD cannot be applied to PEPs, high-risk country relationships, or any
customer flagged during screening.

## 4. Transaction Monitoring

### 4.1 Automated Monitoring

All customer transactions must be subject to automated transaction monitoring
using the firm's approved transaction monitoring system. The system applies
rule-based and behavioural analytics to identify transactions that:
- Are inconsistent with the customer's known profile or stated business purpose
- Involve structuring or smurfing — splitting a transaction to avoid reporting
  thresholds
- Show rapid movement of funds with no apparent commercial rationale
- Involve high-risk jurisdictions, counterparties, or transaction corridors

### 4.2 Alert Handling

Transaction monitoring alerts must be reviewed by a trained AML analyst within
**five (5) business days** of generation. Alerts must be either cleared with
documented rationale or escalated to the Money Laundering Reporting Officer
(MLRO) for SAR assessment. Alerts must not be closed without documented
justification.

### 4.3 Structuring and Threshold Avoidance

Structuring transactions specifically to avoid Currency Transaction Report (CTR)
thresholds or other regulatory reporting requirements is a criminal offence and
a red flag for money laundering. Where structuring is suspected, the transaction
must be held pending MLRO review and a SAR must be filed.

## 5. Suspicious Activity Reports

### 5.1 Internal Reporting

Any employee who knows or suspects that a person is engaged in money laundering
or terrorist financing must submit an internal Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)
to the MLRO promptly — and in any case within **24 hours** of forming that
suspicion. Failure to report is a criminal offence under the Proceeds of Crime
Act 2002.

### 5.2 External SAR Filing

Upon receipt of an internal SAR, the MLRO must assess it and determine within
**seven (7) calendar days** whether to submit an external SAR to the National
Crime Agency (NCA) via the NCA's Suspicious Activity Reports portal. Where a
transaction is pending, a Defence Against Money Laundering (DAML) request must
be submitted before the transaction proceeds.

### 5.3 Tipping Off Prohibition

Once a SAR has been submitted or is under consideration, no employee may
"tip off" the subject of the SAR that a report has been made or is being
considered. Tipping off is a criminal offence under section 333A of the
Proceeds of Crime Act 2002.

### 5.4 Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs)

Cash transactions of **$10,000 or more** (or equivalent) in a single business
day — whether as a single transaction or as multiple transactions that the
firm knows, suspects, or has reason to suspect are related — must be reported
via a Currency Transaction Report to the relevant financial intelligence unit.

## 6. Sanctions Screening

### 6.1 Screening Obligation

All customers, beneficial owners, counterparties, and payment beneficiaries
must be screened against applicable sanctions lists before onboarding and
before processing any payment or transaction. Applicable lists include:
- HM Treasury / OFSI Consolidated List
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
- UN Security Council Consolidated List
- EU Consolidated Sanctions List

Screening must also be repeated at least **daily** for existing customers using
an automated sanctions screening tool approved by the Compliance function.

### 6.2 Positive Matches and Escalation

Any potential match against a sanctions list must be escalated to the MLRO
and the Head of Compliance within **one (1) hour** of identification. No funds
may be transferred to or from a sanctioned person or entity pending resolution
of the match. A confirmed match must be reported to OFSI (UK) or OFAC (US) as
required by the applicable sanctions regime.

### 6.3 Prohibition on Circumvention

Employees must not structure transactions, use intermediaries, or take any
other action designed to circumvent sanctions screening or evade sanctions
obligations. Circumvention of sanctions is a criminal offence and will result
in immediate disciplinary action and regulatory referral.

## 7. Record-Keeping

All KYC records, transaction monitoring alerts, SAR assessments, and sanctions
screening results must be retained for a minimum of **five (5) years** from the
date the business relationship ends or the date of the transaction, whichever
is later. See the Data Retention Policy for full retention schedules.

## 8. Training

All relevant staff must complete AML and sanctions training upon joining and
annually thereafter. Completion records must be maintained by the Compliance
function. Staff who have not completed mandatory training must not be permitted
to perform customer-facing or transaction-processing roles.

## 9. MLRO Responsibilities

The Money Laundering Reporting Officer is the nominated officer under the
Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 and is responsible for:
- Receiving and assessing all internal SARs
- Making decisions on external SAR filing to the NCA
- Maintaining the firm's AML and sanctions risk assessments
- Reporting annually to the Board on the firm's AML and sanctions programme
