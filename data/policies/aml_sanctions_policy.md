# Sanctions & AML Screening Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs the organisation's obligations for anti-money laundering
(AML) and economic sanctions compliance.

## 2. Screening Requirement

All new customer relationships and all transactions above **$10,000** (or
local currency equivalent) must be screened against applicable sanctions
lists (e.g. OFAC, EU consolidated list, UK HMT list) and AML watchlists
prior to processing.

## 3. Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

Where a transaction or pattern of transactions raises reasonable grounds
for suspicion of money laundering, terrorist financing, or sanctions
evasion, a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) must be filed with the
relevant financial intelligence unit within the statutory deadline.
Employees must not disclose to the customer that a SAR has been filed
("tipping off").

## 4. Currency Transaction Reporting (CTR)

Cash transactions exceeding **$10,000** in a single day, or structured to
evade this threshold, must be reported via a Currency Transaction Report
(CTR) within the statutory deadline.

## 5. Prohibited Conduct

No employee may approve, process, or authorise a transaction they know or
suspect is intended to evade sanctions or screening requirements,
regardless of instruction from a manager, client, or other party.
Business pressure or urgency does not override this obligation.

## 6. Ownership

The Anti-Money Laundering (AML) team owns this policy and is the required
routing destination for sanctions/screening questions — not the DPO or
Legal team.
